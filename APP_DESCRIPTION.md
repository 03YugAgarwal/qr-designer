### Designing a Custom QR Code: How It Works

Designing a QR code in Scanly takes less than a minute. Here's the full workflow.

**Step 1 — Enter your content.** Open the Studio tab and type or paste what you want your QR code to contain. A website URL, a phone number, an email address, a Wi-Fi network name and password, a plain message, a vCard contact card — Scanly supports them all. The preview updates in real time, so you see exactly what your code will look like as you type.

**Step 2 — Pick your shapes.** Choose the body shape — the pattern of dots inside your QR code. Options include classic squares, rounded squares, circles, dots, diamonds, and artistic variants. Next, pick your eye frame — the three bigger squares in the corners of every QR code — and the eye ball, the filled shape in the center of each eye. Mix and match to create a code with personality.

**Step 3 — Choose colors or gradients.** Use the color picker to set your body color. Want something more dynamic? Enable a gradient and pick two colors that blend across the code. Set your background color or upload a background image for an extra layer of style. Scanly automatically boosts the error correction level when you add graphical complexity, so your code still scans reliably no matter how creative you get.

**Step 4 — Add a logo.** Tap the logo button to browse Scanly's built-in library of popular logos (perfect for social profiles and common brands), or upload your own PNG or JPG. The logo is placed in the center of the code at a safe, scannable size. Nothing crops, nothing distorts, nothing breaks.

**Step 5 — Add text.** Want to spell out a word in the center of your QR code instead of using a logo? Use the center text field. Want a caption underneath — like "Scan me" or your brand name? Use the caption field. Both are optional.

**Step 6 — Save or export.** Tap Export to get a clean, high-resolution PNG of your design. Use the Save button to add the design to your local Scanly collection so you can come back to edit or re-export it anytime. Share directly to WhatsApp, Gmail, Telegram, Instagram Stories, Drive, or any other app on your device with one tap.

That's it. No tutorials required. The interface guides you through the whole process with a live preview.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Scanly really free?**
Yes. Scanly is free to download, free to use, no hidden purchases. The app doesn't show ads, and there are no watermarks on your exports.

**Do I need an internet connection?**
No. Everything — scanning, generating, exporting, sharing — works fully offline. The only time your phone would use the internet is if you tap an open-link action on a scanned URL and your browser fetches the page.

**Does Scanly collect my data?**
No. Scanly doesn't have a backend, doesn't include analytics, and never sends what you scan or design to any server. Your designs and preferences live on your device and are wiped when you uninstall.

**What kinds of QR codes can I create?**
URLs, plain text, phone numbers, email addresses, Wi-Fi network details, vCards, and more. Scanly automatically picks the right encoding and error correction level for your content.

**Can I use QR codes I create commercially?**
Yes. Your designs are yours. Use them in ads, on products, on packaging, in books, at events, anywhere you want. There are no licensing restrictions from Scanly.

**What image format does Scanly export in?**
PNG, with transparent backgrounds available if you design that way. The resolution is high enough to print on business cards, stickers, posters, or large-format banners.

**Does Scanly work with the QR codes I already have saved on my phone or in my photo library?**
The current version of Scanly focuses on live camera scanning. Scanning an existing image from your gallery is a feature on our roadmap — tell us if you want it sooner.

**Will Scanly ever add ads?**
Scanly is being built as a clean, ad-free experience for this release. In future versions we may introduce a minimal banner ad or an optional "support the developer" contribution, but we'll be transparent about it and never make ads intrusive.

**Does Scanly support bulk QR code generation?**
The initial version focuses on one code at a time — a thoughtfully designed code is usually more valuable than a thousand generic ones. Bulk generation is on our roadmap for a later version.

**Can I customize every part of the QR code?**
Yes. Body shape, eye frame, eye ball, body color, eye colors, background color, background image, gradient, logo, center text, caption — you have control over all of it.

**Is Scanly safe?**
Yes. Scanly doesn't request any dangerous permissions beyond the camera (for scanning) and storage (for saving exported images). It doesn't run background services, doesn't read your location, doesn't touch your contacts, and doesn't listen to your microphone.

**What if the QR code I scan is a scam?**
Scanly shows you what a QR contains before you act on it — so you can read a URL before tapping "Open." If it looks suspicious, don't tap. Scanning a malicious QR on its own can't infect your device; it's only when you choose to visit a link or install something that risk enters the picture.

**Which Android versions are supported?**
Scanly supports Android 7.0 (API 24) and higher.

**What if I find a bug or want a feature?**
Email the developer using the contact link in the Play Store listing. We read every message.

Privacy Policy

Scanly — QR Code Scanner & Generator

Last updated: April 18, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Scanly ("the App", "we", "us") handles information when you use our mobile application. We are committed to protecting your privacy. The App is designed to work primarily on your device — we do not operate servers that collect your personal data.

Short version: Scanly runs entirely on your device. We do not collect, store, or transmit your personal information, QR code contents, or scan history to our servers. Any data the App creates stays on your device unless you choose to share it.

1. Information We Do Not Collect

Scanly does not require an account and does not collect any personally identifiable information such as your name, email address, phone number, or location. We do not maintain a backend server, and we do not track your usage of the App.

2. Data Stored on Your Device

The following data is stored locally on your device and never leaves it unless you explicitly share it:

  • QR code designs you create — names, colors, shapes, and content of QR codes you design.
  • App preferences — theme (light/dark), language, and other settings.
  • Generated QR code images — stored to your device's storage when you save or export them.

Uninstalling the App will remove this locally-stored data.

3. Permissions We Request

The App requests the following permissions, used solely for the features described:

  • Camera — to scan QR codes. Camera frames are processed on-device and are never recorded, uploaded, or transmitted.
  • Storage (Read/Write) — to save generated QR code images to your device and, on older Android versions, to pick logo images you choose to add to your QR designs.
  • Internet — used only to load advertisements (see section 4) and, if applicable, to open external links you tap. The App itself does not send your data over the internet.
  • Vibrate — to provide haptic feedback on a successful scan.

4. Advertising

The App may display advertisements provided by Google AdMob. When ads are shown, AdMob may collect and process certain information in accordance with Google's policies, such as:

  • A device advertising identifier (which you can reset or disable in your device settings).
  • IP address and general device information.
  • Interaction with ads served in the App.

The App requests non-personalized ads where available. For more information about how Google uses this data, see Google's privacy policy at policies.google.com/privacy and the AdMob & AdSense policy at support.google.com/admob/answer/6128543.

5. QR Code Scanning and Generation

When you scan a QR code, the decoded content is displayed only on your device and is not transmitted to us. Similarly, QR codes you generate are created on your device from the content you enter. We do not have access to this content.

If a scanned QR code contains a link and you choose to open it, your device will open that link using your browser or another app — we are not responsible for the privacy practices of those destinations.

6. Children's Privacy

The App is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will take steps to remove it.

7. Third-Party Services

The App includes the following third-party services, which have their own privacy policies:

  • Google AdMob — advertising.
  • Expo — application framework.

8. Security

Because the App stores data only on your device, the security of that data depends on your device's own security settings (such as your screen lock). Sensitive preferences are stored using the platform's secure storage facilities where available.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the App after changes means you accept the updated policy.
